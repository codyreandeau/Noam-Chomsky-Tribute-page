Noam Chomsky

Linguist, Philosopher, Political Activist, Thinker.

Noam Chomsky (1977)

Timeline of Noam Chomsky's Life

  • 1945: Chomsky at age 16 embarks on a general program of study at the University of Pennsylvania. Studied philosophy, logic, and languages.
  • 1947: Chomsky gets introduced to the field of theoretical linguistics.
  • 1951: Graduates with M.A. from Penn.
  • 1951-1955: Chomsky is named into the Society of Fellows at Harvard. Undertakes research for his PhD.
  • 1952: Chomsky publishes first academic paper called "Systems of Syntactic Analysis."
  • 1955: Submits Ph.D thesis on transformational grammar.
  • 1955: Becomes assistant professor at MIT.
  • 1957: Becomes associate professor at MIT, and is employed by Columbia University as a visiting professor.
  • 1959: Published review of B.F. Skinner's book, "Verbal Behavior."
  • 1961: Is awarded academic tenure.
  • 1966-1968: Publishes lots of papers such as, "Aspects of the Theory in Syntax," "Topic in the Theory of Generative Grammar," "Cartesian Linguistics: A Chapter in the History of Rationalist Thought," and "The Sound Pattern of English."
  • 1967: Becomes a public political activist. Is associated with the New Left movement.
  • 1969-1975: Publishes a lot of books. Including "American Power and the New Mandarins," "At War with Asia," "The Backroom Boys," "For Reasons of State," and "Peace in the Middle East?"
  • 1971-1975:Published papers including: "Studies on Semantics and Grammar,"Language and Mind," and "Reflections on Language."
  • 1979: Revised, "Counter-Revolutionary Violence" with Edward Herman.
  • 1988: Chomsky and Herman publishied, "Manufacturing Consent: The Political Economy of the Mass Media."
  • 1990's: Chomsky becomes more politicaly active.
  • 2003: Published, "Hedgemony or Survival."
  • 2008: His wife, Carol Chomsky, passes away.
  • 2016: Releases a documentary called, "Requiem for the American Dream."
